The following represents the nth approximation of a Hilbert curve for a square. With higher values of n, more of the square is filled.

The Hilbert curve is a mapping between 1D and 2D space that preserves locality quite well. This means that points near each other in terms of distance along the curve will also be near each other on the 2D plane.

For example, colours of the rainbow can be plotted such that similar colours are always near each other. Click show colours to see a demonstration below.
